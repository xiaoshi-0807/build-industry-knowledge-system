# Module 2: Competitor Website Deep Dive

## Research Sequence

1. Confirm official domain, market version, and observation date.
2. Inventory navigation, sitemap, collections/categories, product URLs, ordinary pages, and blog/content.
3. Trace at least three user journeys from entry to conversion.
4. Sample products and record offer structure.
5. Analyze trust, claims, proof, objections, and regulatory risk.
6. Analyze SEO/content system and internal linking.
7. Inspect social/account distribution where available.
8. Synthesize business model, strengths, weaknesses, and response options.

## Required Sections

1. Executive summary.
2. Research scope and key public metrics.
3. Navigation and conversion logic.
4. Category/collection architecture.
5. Product architecture and merchandising.
6. Pricing, bundles, subscription, and retention.
7. Claims, trust, proof, reviews, and risk.
8. Content/SEO and internal-link system.
9. Social distribution and content strategy.
10. Business-model reconstruction.
11. Strengths, gaps, and recommended responses.
12. Sources and limitations.

## Minimum Product Sample

Capture:

- Name and URL.
- Format/category and target segment.
- Price, unit economics proxy, bundle, subscription.
- Main claim and proof presented.
- Ingredients/features or core capability.
- Review themes and objections.
- Cross-sells and next step.

## Interpretation Rules

- Treat site structure as evidence of priorities, not proof of revenue.
- Treat “best seller” labels as merchandising claims unless independently verified.
- Do not claim traffic, conversion, or sales without a source.
- Explain the commercial purpose of each observed design choice.
