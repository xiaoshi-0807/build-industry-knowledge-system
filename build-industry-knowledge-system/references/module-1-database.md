# Module 1: Industry Database

## Goal

Create a reusable research database that explains what is sold, who buys, why they buy, what alternatives compete, how demand is expressed, and where opportunity exists.

## Workflow

1. Define the category boundary and adjacent substitutes.
2. Identify regulatory and structural forces.
3. Build eight landscape records.
4. Select 3-8 high-value nodes per dimension for focused records.
5. Connect related records with relative Markdown links or wiki links.
6. Write the root industry map last so its synthesis reflects the database.

## Eight Dimensions

| Dimension | Core question | Required fields |
|---|---|---|
| Brands | Who owns demand and trust? | archetype, audience, hero offer, channel, advantage, weakness |
| Products | What jobs and formats exist? | job, format, mechanism/value story, price, concern |
| Pain-Points | What unresolved problems matter? | trigger, emotional language, workaround, desired outcome |
| Keywords | How is demand expressed in search? | intent, funnel, dominant results, risk, best response |
| Communities | Where do users reveal reality? | behavior, language, recurring topic, research use |
| Competitors | What alternatives solve the same job? | category, why it wins, why it loses, switching trigger |
| Business-Models | How is value captured? | acquisition, conversion, retention, economics, risk |
| Opportunities | Where can someone enter? | signal, target user, thesis, defensibility, risk, validation |

## Required Record Pattern

```yaml
---
title: Record Title
type: dimension-record
industry: Target Industry
market: Target Market
updated: YYYY-MM-DD
status: active
sources:
  - https://example.com
---
```

Each record must answer:

- What is it?
- Why does it matter?
- What evidence supports the conclusion?
- What does it connect to?
- What remains uncertain?

## Quality Gate

- Avoid broad encyclopedia descriptions.
- Capture user language, commercial mechanics, and decision relevance.
- Include adjacent and non-obvious substitutes in competitors.
- Make opportunities traceable to demand signals and market gaps.
