# Module 4: Knowledge Map

## Goal

Turn the database into a decision map that shows structure, relationships, competition, growth, gaps, and opportunity.

## Three-Level Structure

- Level 1: Industry.
- Level 2: 6-10 structural layers or major markets.
- Level 3: Categories/capabilities within each layer.
- Optional Level 4: Specific use cases, products, or opportunity nodes.

Use layers appropriate to the industry. Common choices:

- Inputs/supply.
- Product/service categories.
- Distribution and channels.
- Users and jobs.
- Operations and enabling infrastructure.
- Regulation and risk.
- Measurement and economics.
- Business models.

## Required Labels

Define a small, explicit legend. Example:

- `High competition`
- `Fast growth`
- `Content/research gap`
- `High regulatory risk`

Do not assign labels without explaining the evidence or inference behind them.

## Required Sections

1. Boundary and database coverage.
2. Label legend.
3. Three-level directory tree.
4. Core dependency chain.
5. Relationship table.
6. Feedback loops and risk loops.
7. Prioritized opportunities.
8. Areas to avoid or enter cautiously.
9. Metrics and signals to track continuously.
10. Sources and limitations.

## Opportunity Test

Every prioritized opportunity must identify:

- Specific underserved user and job.
- Demand signal.
- Why now.
- Why incumbents underserve it.
- Defensible entry wedge.
- Main risk.
- Smallest validation action.
