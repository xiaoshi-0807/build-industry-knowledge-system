# Module 3: Top Social Accounts And Content

## Define The Universe

State:

- Platform and market/language.
- Top N ranking source or selection rule.
- Whether the universe is brands, creators, experts, communities, or all.
- Collection date and accessible sample size.
- Metric definitions.

## Account-Level Fields

`rank`, `handle`, `url`, `followers/subscribers`, `positioning`, `audience`, `primary content job`, `posting frequency`, `monetization path`, `data limitation`.

## Content-Level Fields

`content_id`, `url`, `author`, `published_at`, `title/hook`, `format`, `duration`, `views`, `likes`, `comments`, `shares`, `saves`, `topic`, `content job`, `CTA`.

Use a consistent sample such as each account's latest 20 accessible posts. Report missing accounts.

## Recommended Content Jobs

| Job | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Exposure | Earn reach through strong claims, novelty, or debate |
| Growth | Convert reach into follows with repeatable value |
| Save | Provide checklists, tutorials, recipes, or reference value |
| Persona | Build trust through identity, story, and worldview |
| Conversion | Move high-intent users toward a product or service |

Adapt names when another taxonomy better fits the platform.

## Analysis

- Compare mean and median performance by content job.
- Identify breakout content by views, engagement, shares, and saves where available.
- Analyze hook, length, narrative, proof, visual format, CTA, and target identity.
- Separate repeatable structures from one-off celebrity or news effects.
- Translate findings into content pillars, monthly mix, and reusable templates.

## Boundaries

- Engagement does not prove sales or efficacy.
- Public rankings are a proxy sample, not the entire market.
- Do not compare metrics from incompatible time windows without qualification.
- For trend tracking, compare metric growth between snapshots, not only totals.
