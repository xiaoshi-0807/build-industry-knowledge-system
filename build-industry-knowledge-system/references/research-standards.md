# Research Standards

## Scope Contract

Write these fields before collecting data:

| Field | Required decision |
|---|---|
| Industry | Define included and excluded categories |
| Market | Geography, language, regulatory jurisdiction |
| Customer | Buyer, user, and decision maker |
| Objective | Investment, product, content, sales, or general learning |
| Time horizon | Current snapshot, historical change, or future opportunity |
| Output language | User preference |

## Evidence Hierarchy

1. Regulators, government datasets, standards bodies, court filings.
2. Company websites, product pages, filings, help centers, press releases.
3. Peer-reviewed research and recognized research institutions.
4. Platform-native account and content pages.
5. Reputable trade publications and specialist newsletters.
6. Third-party aggregators and rankings.
7. Community posts and reviews.

Use lower levels for discovery and language. Do not use them alone for high-stakes claims.

## Fact Discipline

- Add `updated` or `observed_at` dates to volatile records.
- Use exact dates instead of relative time.
- Mark approximate numbers with `~` or “约”.
- Cite source links for rankings, audience counts, prices, market sizes, regulations, and strategic claims.
- Never present inaccessible internal metrics as facts.
- Label modeled market sizes, likely traffic sources, or commercial intent as estimates/inferences.
- Distinguish ingredient evidence from finished-product evidence.

## Analysis Discipline

Every major conclusion should contain:

1. Signal or observation.
2. Interpretation.
3. Business implication.
4. Confidence or limitation.
5. Next validation action when uncertainty is material.

## Opportunity Scoring

Score 1-5:

| Dimension | High score means |
|---|---|
| Pain intensity | Frequent, costly, emotionally strong problem |
| Willingness to pay | Clear budget or existing spend |
| Growth | Demand or enabling change is accelerating |
| Evidence feasibility | Outcome can be credibly demonstrated |
| Channel fit | Reachable through available distribution |
| Repeatability | Recurrent usage or recurring revenue |
| Defensibility | Data, expertise, trust, workflow, or community moat |
| Compliance risk | Reverse score: lower risk earns higher score |

Do not hide weak dimensions behind a single total. Explain the tradeoff.
